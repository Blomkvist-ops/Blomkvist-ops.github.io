Freedom, fear and coward

I accidentally found Nathan Law’s new book had been published. I’ve heard of his name before, but not in some good news. Together with his fellows, he led a movement in HK.

You cannot simply judge or make comments on this kind of thing. Since political judgment is extremely sensitive there. Although I’ve come a long way from a poor peasant city where marijuana is forbidden to a rich peasant city with a developed marijuana industry, I still fear the power that censors everything you say and see.

Snowden is one of the people I admire most. His biography, permanent record, reveals the censorship in America, which is horrible enough. As is known to all, censorship in China can only be more serious. Brave and intelligent as Snowden can barely handle this government shit, I think my fear after reading that book is understandable. But, after all, this happened in the US, which is far away from my safe, prosperous and flourishing mother country.

Law’s problem, however, is apparently more realistic and related to me. But I cannot comment on this because I’m still afraid of it despite I’m in a distant city where you can smell marijuana on the streets, buses, and campus.

I have read this book. That is all I can say. I’m a coward.

Freedom, what a beautiful word. I dream of it every single day. But where is it? Where can I find it? I used to think if I’m in a different country where nobody knows me, I can get the freedom I want. The truth is, here I am, I still feel the inevitable fear and the desperate connection.

I’m a coward. But I love freedom. I do not dare to fight for it. Somebody does. BTW, I mean Snowden, not anyone else.
